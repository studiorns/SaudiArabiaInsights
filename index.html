Saudi Arabia Market Metrics & Insights

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

95.40% (+2.40pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 95.40%, Saudi Arabia has near-universal awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating market saturation for basic awareness.
  • The +2.40pp year-over-year growth suggests continued improvement despite already high levels.
  • Marketing focus should shift from basic awareness to differentiation and consideration, as nearly everyone already knows about the destination.
  • Budget allocation should prioritize conversion rather than awareness-building campaigns.

Consideration

61.47% (+1.54pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 61.47%, consideration is good but indicates that about 39% of aware consumers would not consider Abu Dhabi for their next vacation.
  • The +1.54pp growth shows positive momentum but at a slower rate than awareness growth.
  • This suggests that while more people know about Abu Dhabi, converting that awareness to consideration remains challenging.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing barriers to consideration, particularly the top barriers identified in our research.

Intent

46.30% (+0.56pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 46.30%, intent is good but shows that less than half of those considering Abu Dhabi have strong intentions to visit soon.
  • The modest +0.56pp growth indicates slower momentum at this stage of the funnel.
  • This suggests that while consideration is growing, converting consideration to actual intent to visit remains challenging.
  • Marketing should focus on creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking (value perception, competing destinations, etc.).

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.75 (-0.01)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 46.30% ÷ 61.47% = 0.75

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.75, the conversion from consideration to intent is good, indicating that 75% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The slight decline of -0.01 suggests a minor decrease in conversion efficiency.
  • This indicates that while both consideration and intent are growing, consideration is growing slightly faster than intent.
  • Marketing should focus on closing the gap between consideration and intent through targeted campaigns addressing specific barriers to booking.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Saudi Arabia market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

6 days

The average number of nights Saudi travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 6-day average stay indicates a medium-length vacation market, longer than a weekend getaway but shorter than an extended holiday.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on itineraries and experiences that fit within a 5-7 day window.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this timeframe to maximize appeal.
  • There may be opportunity to extend stays by 1-2 days through targeted promotions and itinerary suggestions.

Booking Window

24 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Saudi travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 24-day booking window is relatively short, indicating a spontaneous travel market with last-minute decision-making.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 3-5 weeks before target travel periods.
  • Promotional offers should have quick expiration dates to create urgency.
  • Flexibility in booking policies and last-minute availability are important for this market.
  • Digital and social media channels are likely more effective than traditional media due to the short decision timeframe.
Strategic Example:

For the summer peak season (July-August), major campaign pushes should begin in late May to early June to align with the 24-day booking window. Flash sales and limited-time offers would be particularly effective for this market.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Jul & Aug

The months with the highest volume of Saudi hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Saudi guests.

Business Implications:
  • July (29,809 guests) and August (22,492 guests) represent the clear peak season for Saudi travelers.
  • This summer peak aligns with school holidays and vacation periods in Saudi Arabia.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (May-June, September) to extend the peak period.
  • Winter months represent a growth opportunity through targeted campaigns highlighting Abu Dhabi's pleasant winter climate.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

1.4 (moderate seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Saudi Arabia travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if July has a travel query index of 25.01 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 17.86, the seasonality index would be approximately 1.4 (25.01 ÷ 17.86).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 1.4 value indicates that the market has moderate seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 40% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant seasonal marketing strategies but not as extreme as some markets that might have values of 2.0 or higher.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 24-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern but not be exclusively focused on peak periods.

Media Elasticity

0.42 (moderate impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Saudi Arabia market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.42 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 4.2%.
  • This indicates that media investments do drive travel queries, but with diminishing returns.
  • The relationship is positive but not proportional (less than 1.0).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI, but with moderate efficiency.
  • Doubling media spend will not double travel queries.
  • The marketing team should focus on improving creative quality and targeting to increase this elasticity.
  • This moderate elasticity suggests that other factors beyond media (such as word-of-mouth, travel restrictions, or competing destinations) also significantly influence travel decisions.
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 20% for a campaign, you can expect approximately an 8.4% increase in travel queries (20% × 0.42 = 8.4%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.65 (moderate consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Saudi Arabia travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.65 consistency indicates that while there are some variations in growth rates across different months, there's also a moderate level of predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with moderate confidence.
  • Some months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Other months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams should maintain some flexibility in budget allocation.
  • Historical patterns provide useful guidance but shouldn't be relied upon exclusively.
  • This moderate consistency (0.65) contributes to the forecast confidence interval of ±6.8%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±6.8%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±6.8% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 20.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 18.64 and 21.36 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reasonably reliable forecast for the Saudi Arabia market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The moderate confidence interval suggests that while forecasts are useful for planning, real-time monitoring and agile response remain important.
Strategic Example:

When planning media budgets for July 2025 based on a forecast of 25.25 travel queries, you should prepare for scenarios where the actual value could range from 23.53 to 26.97. This might mean having contingency plans for both underperformance and overperformance within this range.

Impression Efficiency

0.68 queries per 1000 impressions

Measures how efficiently media impressions generate travel queries, indicating the effectiveness of our creative content and targeting.

How It's Calculated:

Total travel queries divided by total impressions (in thousands) over a specific time period.

Formula: Impression Efficiency = Travel Queries ÷ (Impressions ÷ 1000)

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low efficiency, poor conversion of impressions to queries
  • 0.5-0.8: Moderate efficiency, reasonable conversion of impressions
  • 0.8-1.2: Good efficiency, strong conversion of impressions
  • >1.2: Excellent efficiency, very strong conversion of impressions
Business Implications:
  • At 0.68 queries per 1000 impressions, the Saudi Arabia market shows moderate efficiency in converting media exposure to actual travel interest.
  • This suggests that while our creative content and targeting are reasonably effective, there's room for improvement.
  • Marketing should focus on optimizing creative content and targeting to increase this efficiency.
  • A/B testing different creative approaches could help identify more effective messaging.
  • Audience segmentation and more precise targeting could also improve this metric.
  • Monitoring this metric over time can help identify trends in creative effectiveness and audience responsiveness.

Queries-to-Bookings Ratio

1:38 (stable YoY)

Measures the relationship between travel queries and actual bookings, indicating the efficiency of our conversion funnel.

How It's Calculated:

The ratio of travel queries to actual bookings over a specific time period, typically calculated by dividing the number of bookings by the travel query index and expressing as a ratio.

Business Implications:
  • The 1:38 ratio indicates that for every 1 point increase in the travel query index, we see approximately 38 additional bookings.
  • The stability year-over-year suggests a consistent relationship between search interest and conversion.
  • This stable ratio allows for reliable forecasting of bookings based on travel query predictions.
  • Marketing can use this ratio to set specific query targets based on desired booking outcomes.
  • Any significant changes in this ratio should be investigated as they may indicate issues in the conversion funnel or changes in consumer behavior.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Saudi travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q1 2021):
  • Already Visited: 50% (-3pp) have already visited
  • Not Value for Money: 35% (+14pp) find it not value for money
  • Other Places to Visit: 29% (+18pp) would rather visit other places
  • Not Much to Do: 24% (-1pp) feel there's not much to do
  • Too Expensive: 24% (-14pp) find it too expensive
Business Implications:
  • Already Visited (50%, -3pp): The high percentage indicates market saturation, but the slight decrease suggests some improvement. Marketing should focus on repeat visitation strategies highlighting new attractions and experiences.
  • Not Value for Money (35%, +14pp): The significant increase is concerning and suggests deteriorating value perception. Marketing should emphasize value propositions and potentially develop value-oriented packages.
  • Other Places to Visit (29%, +18pp): The substantial increase indicates growing competition from alternative destinations. Marketing should emphasize Abu Dhabi's unique differentiators and experiences unavailable elsewhere.
  • Not Much to Do (24%, -1pp): This perception remains relatively stable. Marketing should continue to highlight the diversity of activities and experiences available in Abu Dhabi.
  • Too Expensive (24%, -14pp): The significant decrease is positive, suggesting improved price perception. However, this should be viewed alongside the increased "Not Value for Money" perception, indicating that while price concerns have decreased, value concerns have increased.
Strategic Recommendation:

Based on these barriers, a "What's New in Abu Dhabi" campaign targeting repeat visitors (addressing the 50% "Already Visited" barrier) combined with a "More for Your Money" value proposition (addressing the 35% "Not Value for Money" barrier) would address the two most significant barriers to visitation from the Saudi Arabia market.

Decreasing Barriers

4 improving factors

Barriers to visitation that have shown significant improvement over time, representing successful perception changes.

Improving Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q1 2021):
  • Bad Experiences: 1% (-20pp) heard others had bad experiences
  • Too Expensive: 24% (-14pp) find it too expensive
  • Laws and Customs: 9% (-5pp) find laws/customs too restrictive
  • Safety Concerns: 5% (-5pp) perceive it as unsafe
Business Implications:
  • The dramatic decrease in "Bad Experiences" (-20pp) suggests significant improvements in visitor satisfaction and word-of-mouth.
  • The substantial decrease in "Too Expensive" perception (-14pp) indicates successful communication of pricing and offers.
  • The improvements in perceptions around laws, customs, and safety suggest successful educational marketing about Abu Dhabi's visitor-friendly environment.
  • These positive changes should be maintained and leveraged in marketing communications.
  • Resources previously focused on addressing these improving barriers can be redirected to address the increasing barriers like "Not Value for Money" and "Other Places to Visit."

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for Saudi Arabia. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Saudi Arabia market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Near-universal awareness (95.40%) but moderate consideration (61.47%) and intent (46.30%)
  • A short booking window (24 days) indicating spontaneous travel decisions
  • Strong summer seasonality with peaks in July and August
  • Moderate media elasticity (0.42) suggesting reasonable but not exceptional response to media investments
  • Significant barriers around repeat visitation (50% already visited) and value perception (35% not value for money)
  • Strong interest in cultural attractions, particularly Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, followed by family entertainment options
  • Preference for luxury accommodations, with Dusit Thani Abu Dhabi showing exceptional popularity
  • Diverse location preferences spanning city center, Yas Island, and Saadiyat Island properties

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Developing compelling reasons for repeat visitation, highlighting new experiences at popular attractions
  • Improving value perception through targeted offers and packages
  • Timing campaigns to align with the short booking window
  • Optimizing creative content and targeting to improve media efficiency
  • Developing winter season campaigns to balance the seasonal distribution
  • Creating marketing content that highlights both cultural significance and family entertainment options
  • Developing bundled packages that combine cultural attractions with entertainment venues
  • Partnering with top-viewed accommodations, particularly Dusit Thani Abu Dhabi, for co-marketing initiatives
  • Showcasing luxury accommodation options alongside cultural attractions in marketing materials

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the Saudi Arabia market.
